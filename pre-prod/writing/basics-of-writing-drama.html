The Basics of Writing Drama

Article by Harry

Every film needs the basics of drama.

1. Two Main Types of Drama

a.) Tragedies-Serious plays generally ending in suffering and death.
b.) Comedies-Lighter acts that tend to be funny.

2. Elements of Play

a.) Plot-The sequence of related events that make up the story;puts characters against one another or against powerful forces.
b.) Stage Directions-Gives actors and actresses lines on where to go on stage and how to feel. Also can be known as a Narrator.
c.) Set-A realistic representation of the play's setting.
d.) Props-Representations of the play's setting.
e.) Exposition-Usually begins a drama; explains the action that has already occurred.

3. Order of Events (In a basic drama)

a.) Conflict-Difficulties that the characters must overcome.
b.) Climax-Comes at the end of an act, the big part the changes everything.
c.) Resolution-End of drama; the conflict is solved whether it is tragic or a happy ending.

Remember this is a basic guide to drama, for more advanced drama you still need to base it around this core.
