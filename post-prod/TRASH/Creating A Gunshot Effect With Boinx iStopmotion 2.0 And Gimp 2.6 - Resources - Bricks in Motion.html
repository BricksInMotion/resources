Creating A Gunshot Effect With Boinx iStopmotion 2.0 And Gimp 2.6
Article by J. (Edit/Delete/Back to draft)

A brief guide on how to create a gunshot effect with Gimp 2.6 and iStopmotion.

First, open up your iStopmotion document. Click on "iStopmotion" in the top bar, then go to "Preferences", then "Helpers". It should look like this:

Go back to your document. Select the frame right when the gun "Kicks". Click "Edit", "Edit In Gimp". It should pull up your frame. Select the paint tool. It looks like this:
Paint a small Red blob in front of the gun, like so:

Select the smudge tool. It looks like this:
Smudge the blob until it look like this:

Next, select Orange as the color. Paint a smaller Orange  blob in front of the gun and smudge it until it looks like this:

Next, select Yellow as the color. Paint an even smaller Yellow blob in front of the gun and smudge it until it looks like this:

Next, select White as the color. Paint a smaller still White blob in front of the gun and smudge it until it finally looks like this:

Then, close, save, and export the image. When you go back to iStopmotion, the finished image should appear in the old, un-edited frame's place.
