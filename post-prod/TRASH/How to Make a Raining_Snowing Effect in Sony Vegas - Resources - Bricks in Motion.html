How to Make a Raining/Snowing Effect in Sony Vegas
Article by Tobias Nikl (Edit/Delete/Back to draft)

A short tutorial on how to easily make a raining/snowing effect in Sony Vegas.

If you'd like to create some nice raining/snowing effects, you can now easily in Sony Vegas.
If you do not know what Sony Vegas is, it's very good and simple editing software that most brickfilmers use. You may download it and have a license for a month, and then you can just easily buy it (not very expensive, usually around 50 dollars).
So now that you have gotten Sony Vegas, and you know how to use it, I will show you how to create a very nice raining/snowing effect.

Step 1

So first off, as usual, make a new project; you do that by clicking on "Project" > "New...".
Now, just simply insert your clip into the timeline. You do this by clicking on "Project" > "Import Media...". Your clip should appear in the "Project Media" section, and to put it in the timeline you just simply drag it into the "Video" section in the timeline.

Step 2

The next thing you want to do, is to go to your "Media Generators" section, pick the "Starry Sky" icon, and drag it into the "Video Overlay" section in your timeline, so it's right above your clip. Now, as you have dragged that into your timeline, a setting for the "Starry Sky" icon should appear. And you just want to play with the colors and stuff like that (I suggest making the color of the dots white). Below the settings of the color of the dots, there should be a color B icon - you want to drag that to the lowest, to make the black background transparent.

Now, still in the "Starry Sky" settings, if you want it to be a snow effect, you can leave it just as the dots. But if you want it to be a raining effect, then you need obviously make the dots looking like drops of water. To do that, you need to play a bit with the settings on the left side.

Step 4

And once the rain looks like you want it to be, in your timeline, make the raining clip's length the same as your clip's. Then, still in the "Starry Sky" settings, you need to drag the line in the "Noise Texture" timeline (in the "Starry Sky" settings) all the way to the end, and drag the line in "Progress (in degrees)" all the way to the end.

Now you should be pretty much finished. Although, if your clip isn't as big as the raining clip, (like in these picture examples) then just simply make the raining clip size smaller. You do that by clicking on the "Event/Pan Crop" icon that is on your raining clip in the timeline, and make it smaller in the settings.

Usefull Suggestions

I strongly suggest when you're filming a raining scene that you put some drops water on your set and minifigures, because it makes the film look more realistic. If it's with snow, then use something that looks like snow (sugar etc.). Cleaning it up isn't usually a problem at all, mainly not with water. And a lots of brickfilmers actually use this technique, if they're doing a raining/snowing scene (ex: Unrenewable by Philip Heinrich, The Magician's Hat by Jack Bond). I also suggest making the lighting a bit darker, as when it's raining it's not exactly sunny outside.

So that should be about it. After you're done with the clip, just view it and simply render it.

Thank you for reading, and have a good day!

P.S. The animation example in the pictures wasn't a test for raining/snowing effect.
