Making Lightsabers In Photoshop
Article by BuilderBrothers (Edit/Delete/Back to draft)

A short tutorial that tells how to make lightsabers in Photoshop.

First, open Photoshop, and once in Photoshop, open the frame from your animation that you wish to add a lightsaber effect to.  For this tutorial, we will be using this image:

Second, make two new layers.   Name the bottom layer “glow” and the top layer “blade.”  Then, select layer “blade” and, using an air brush, draw a white line over the blade of the lightsaber.  Make sure the entire blade is covered.  You should get something like this:

Third, select the layer “glow” and, using a slightly larger air brush than before, draw a line that is whatever color you want the lightsaber to be.  In this case, it would be light blue.  Doing this, and you should get something similar to this:

Fourth, make the background layer invisible, and go to Layer/Merge Visible (Shift+Ctrl+E).  This makes both the glow and the blade one layer.  Rename this new layer “lightsaber.”  Last of all, to add to the realism of the blade, select layer “lightsaber” and go to Filter/Render/Lens Flare.  Select the lens type “105mm Prime” and adjust the brightness to about 120.  Line up the lens flare to the place where the lightsaber meets the handle.  Then click OK.  This gives the bottom of the blade a look of high-intensity.  You should get something like this:

You are done!  Now all you have to do is open all the frames in your animation that have lightsabers in them, and then simply copy and reposition the layer “lightsaber” onto the blade in each shot.  If you have a blade in a different color, then simply adjust the hue/saturation of the lightsaber.  After a little practice, you’ll have it down easily!
