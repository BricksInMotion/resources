Masking in Gimp
Article by Mason Muszynski (Edit/Delete/Back to draft)

For all you brickfilmers who do not have the pleasure of having Photoshop, there is still another way to mask. This technique takes some time, since it goes through every frame, but will have a spectacular outcome.

Table of Contents
What You'll Need
Procedure

What You'll Need

  • Gimp ............... http://www.gimp.org

  • Patience

  • "Blank" Frame

  • "Rigged" Frame

Procedure

1. Open up your background image into  gimp.

2. Next, Go File > Open as layers and Open up your rigged frame.

3. In your layers section, select the “rigged” photo and go to Layers > Mask > Add Layer Mask.
4.  Select ‘White (Full Opacity)’ And click the ‘Add’ button.
5. Next Go Layers > Mask > Apply Layer Mask
6. Now select the free select tool And trace the minifigure. Zoom in for detail

7. Now  go to your tool options, and check feathered edges. Adjust to your liking (which depends on your frame resolution). Feathered edges will make the photo look more ‘Natural’. Go to Select > Invert, then press the delete button on your key board.

8. Now go to Select > None and go back with the eraser and free select tools and go back and get rid of what you missed.

9. Now we must create a shadow so it looks realistic. Create a New Layer and set it to ‘Transparency’. Then select the airbrush tool And set the brush to ‘Circle Fuzzy’  Now create a shadow roughly the length of the minifigure floating in the air. Once you’ve done this, that is your final product!

Now repeat all of those steps with every frame of your animation that you wish to be masked, or also know as “Flying Lego”.

Happy Masking!
